# Medical anthropologist, university professor in training 
## Cognitive scientist specialized in psychological anthropology, ethnopsychiatry, social approaches to mental health, and human biocultural diversity with applications in medicine.

I am a doctoral researcher at the Medical Anthropology Research Center, Universitat Rovira i Virgili (Tarragona, Spain), working at the interface between social and health sciences. My doctoral work, now approaching completion after nearly six years, has focused on violence and iatrogenic harm in mental health settings, with particular emphasis on eliminating coercion through patient and stakeholder engagement within an action research framework.

I have been concurrently pursuing training as a physician scientist, undertaking undergraduate studies in medicine and medical bioengineering at the Universitat de Barcelona and the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. The medical track of this trajectory has been placed on hold in order to prioritize intensive fieldwork and in response to severe adverse circumstances and abuses experienced during this period. I intend to resume and complete this training as conditions allow.

My research is funded by the Spanish Ministry of Universities through a Professor in Training grant (FPU 2019) until October 2026. Alongside this work, I contribute to the development of the Open Science Commons Cloud, an open infrastructure for collaborative research, education, and knowledge translation, with a focus on interoperability, distributed systems, and the integration of open science practices across domains.

My previous research experience includes work at the Stanford Center for Legal Informatics (CodeX) at Stanford Law School, the James E. Rogers College of Law at the University of Arizona, and the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies in Geneva. There, I focused on social network analysis and cognitive biases in decision making processes within international arbitration systems, including the World Trade Organization dispute settlement body.

More recently, I conducted research on the genetic and neurobiological dimensions of the schizophrenia construct, including the role of the neuritin gene (NRN1) and associated neurotrophic factors in cortical structure, at the FIDMAG Research Foundation within the Benito Menni Mental Health Care Complex.

My academic background includes studies in neuroscience and smart systems at Keele University, evolutionary anthropology at Universitat Rovira i Virgili and Universitat Oberta de Catalunya, and a master’s degree in biological anthropology with specialization in psychiatric genetics and human diversity at the Universitat de Barcelona and Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. I have also completed postgraduate training in collective mental health and dialogical practices.

I seek collaboration with researchers, practitioners, public institutions, and civic initiatives engaged in advancing open, interdisciplinary, and socially grounded approaches to knowledge production, with the aim of translating research into improved policy and practice.
